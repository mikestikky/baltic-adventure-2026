Flights

Ground legs

Driving notes — Latvia & Lithuania

  • Side / rules: Drive on the right. Headlights on at all times (low beam, day and night) — required in both countries.
  • Documents: Carry passport, driving licence, the rental agreement, and proof of insurance in the car.
  • Tolls / vignette: No motorway vignette needed for cars in Latvia or Lithuania (unlike some EU states) — but confirm with EconomyBookings at pickup.
  • Border: Latvia → Lithuania is a Schengen internal border — no stop, no checkpoint. Just confirm the rental contract allows the cross-border drive (open item).
  • Fuel: Petrol stations (Circle K, Neste, Viada) are common on the A-roads. Return the car with a full tank to avoid refuelling charges.
  • Old Towns: Riga & Vilnius Old Towns are largely pedestrian/restricted — park at the hotel garage or a nearby lot, don't drive in to the door.
The heart of the trip

Stumble walks

Not checklists. Wander zones — the few streets and doorways where beauty tends to ambush you. Walk slowly, follow the open door, let one lane lead to the next.

Tallinn — the medieval wander zone

Your base, the Telegraaf, sits right inside it. Give Tallinn one truly unplanned day (Jul 11) and let it work.

The lanes

  • Vene Street — the hotel's own street; oldest, most atmospheric spine.
  • St. Catherine's Passage (Katariina käik) — the postcard alley; best at dusk and after dark.
  • Pikk Street — guild houses, the old merchants' road up toward the towers.
  • Town Hall Square (Raekoja plats) — and the tiny lanes (Saiakang) feeding off it, where the cellar cafés hide.
  • Toompea viewpoints — Kohtuotsa & Patkuli, for the red roofs from above.

Happy-accident pins

Café Maiasmokk · Pierre Chocolaterie (Master's Courtyard) · Kehrwieder (Saiakang lane) · Kofeman · Loulou (breakfast) · Schlössle Hotel fireplace lounge (drop in as non-guests) · the Telegraaf bar.

Riga — two textures in one Old Town

Riga is the peak "unplanned jaw-dropping room" city. It has two distinct moods; wander between them on Jul 15 and reserve only Rozengrāls.

Texture one — Art Nouveau facades

  • Alberta iela — the most extravagant street; look up.
  • Elizabetes iela — more of the same grandeur a block over.
  • Art Café Sienna — coffee among period rooms while you're up there.

Texture two — Old Town cellars

  • Rozengrāls — a 13th-century candlelit cellar (the one to reserve).
  • Riga Black Magic — theatrical dark bar built on Riga Black Balsam.
  • House of the Black Heads — the fairy-tale facade on Town Hall Square.
  • Grand Palace Hotel bar — your own old-world nightcap room.

Vilnius — magic behind the archways

Vilnius doesn't show off on the street; it hides courtyards behind gates. Push the doors open.

The spine & the secrets

  • Pilies Street — the main artery; the good stuff is down the side lanes off it.
  • Courtyards off Pilies & Didžioji — including the hotel PACAI's own hidden courtyard.
  • Užupis — cross the little bridge into the self-declared artists' republic; find the constitution on the wall, the galleries, the river benches.
  • Cathedral Square — the grand open end of the Old Town.

Happy-accident pins

Eskedar Coffee Bar · Italala Caffe · Augustas & Barbora Love Story Café · Užupis wine bars · Grand Hotel Vilnius afternoon tea (if it fits) · PACAI courtyard.

Good to know

Practical guide

The boring-but-important layer, in one place.

Weather — July in the Baltics

Mild and long-lit. Highs usually 19–24 °C (66–75 °F), cool evenings around 12–14 °C. Daylight is very long (sun up ~4:30 AM, down ~10:30 PM). Pack layers and a light rain jacket — showers come and go.

Money & cards

All four countries use the euro (€) — no currency changes en route. Cards are accepted almost everywhere; carry a little cash for small cafés and markets. Tell your bank you're traveling. ATMs (Otto., Luminor, SEB) are easy to find.

Power & plugs

All four use Type C / F plugs, 230 V. Bring a EU adapter (and ideally a small multi-USB charger). US dual-voltage electronics (phones, laptops) just need the plug adapter, no converter.

Passport & Schengen

Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania are all in the Schengen area — one entry stamp at Helsinki, then no border checks between countries. Passports valid 6+ months past return. (EU's new ETIAS may apply for US visitors — check status before travel.)

Phone & data

An EU eSIM (Airalo / Holafly) covering all four countries is the cheapest path, or confirm your carrier's international plan. Download offline Google Maps for FI/EE/LV/LT before you go (open item).

Health & emergencies

Emergency number across the EU is 112. Pharmacies = "Apteekki / Apteek / Aptieka / Vaistinė." Bring any prescriptions in original packaging + a small kit (pain relief, blister plasters for the cobblestones, motion-sickness for the ferry).

Travel insurance

Confirm a policy that covers medical + trip interruption + the rental car gap (if not relying on a credit-card CDW). Save the policy number offline.

Copies & backups

Keep this guide saved offline (or printed). Photograph passports, both tickets, all hotel confirmations, and the car booking; store in a shared album you can both reach.
